EAD 871: Collegiate Contexts for Teaching & Learning

Course Details

Semester: Fall 2023

Format: Online

Credits: 3.0

Grade: 3.0

Instructor: Dr. Kenya Messer, Ed.D.

Course Description

This course examines the connection between collegiate contexts and teaching and learning, where contexts include physical space, temporality, academic disciplines, geography, and the broader socio-political milieu that shapes how colleges and universities are designed and supported. The course moves beyond formal classroom instruction to consider how students develop understanding through interactions across residence halls, organizational meetings, student demonstrations, work environments, and online settings.

Course Objectives

Teaching & Learning Contexts

Developed a deeper understanding of what characterizes teaching and learning in collegiate contexts and the various sites — both formal and informal — where learning takes place beyond the traditional classroom.

Sociocultural Forces

Examined the conditions and forces that shape sites of learning, including physical space, temporality, affect, disciplinary structures, and the hidden curriculum operating within higher education institutions.

Student Persistence

Explored how varying paradigms of teaching and learning influence students' persistence in their learning environments, with attention to retention theory and its application to diverse student populations.

Critical & Scholarly Analysis

Strengthened skills as an analytical reader and conceptual writer, learning to make argument-driven claims using relevant evidence and to think critically about whose perspectives and assumptions inform scholarship on teaching and learning.

Course Structure

Module 1: Conceptualizing Teaching & Learning

Explored foundational teaching perspectives, learning theories, and sites of learning beyond the classroom. Engaged with scholarship on andragogy, embodied learning, informal learning spaces, service-learning, and the experiences of first-generation students. Completed the Pratt Teaching Perspectives Inventory to identify personal teaching orientations.

Module 2: Sociocultural Contexts

Examined the invisible and taken-for-granted social forces shaping collegiate learning environments: physical space, temporality, the hidden curriculum, disciplinary contexts, and the impact of online and post-COVID learning. Analyzed how these forces operate across institutional settings.

Module 3: Student Persistence

Synthesized course content to explore outcomes of teaching and learning and their impacts on student persistence. Engaged with retention scholarship including faculty-student relationships, active learning, learning communities, and models specific to minority-serving institutions and community colleges.

Major Assignments

Movie Analysis

Wrote an argument-driven essay analyzing two recurring themes of teaching and learning as represented in a collegiate-context film, synthesizing at least five Module 1 readings to demonstrate comprehension of teaching perspectives and sites of learning applied to a real-world scenario.

Novel Analysis

Analyzed two to three sociocultural forces at play in a chosen novel set within a collegiate context, drawing on Module 2 scholarship on space, time, affect, and disciplinary structures to construct an evidence-based argument about the invisible forces shaping learning environments.

Topic Paper: Case Study / Shadow Experience

Completed a case study or shadow experience illustrating how systems, departments, individuals, or programs impact student persistence in college. Synthesized Module 3 content to analytically connect teaching and learning contexts to persistence outcomes.

Discussion Posts & Visual Reflection

Participated in modular discussion forums engaging with reading questions and peer responses. Concluded the course with a reflective visual exercise comparing initial and evolved understandings of collegiate contexts for teaching and learning.
